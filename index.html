AI Researcher

Current Pre-doc Fellow at The University of Chicago Booth in Quantitative Marketing with Prof. Andreas Kraft. I'm applying for PhD programs in CS or Quant Marketing for the Fall 2027 cycle!

I'm broadly interested in decision-making and the nature of intelligence. My research publications/preprints span AI Interpretability, AI Consciousness, and Choice Modeling. Details can be found on my Google Scholar.

The theme of my research is using insights from human choice behavior to guide the design and understanding of AI, while leveraging AI advances to reveal and enhance how we make choices.

In past lives, I earned my M.S. in Data Science from The University of Chicago (GPA 4.0), triple-majored in Economics, Finance, and History at The Ohio State University (top 1% Honors), worked as a research assistant for behavioral scientist Dr. Nick Epley (UChicago) and historian Dr. Christopher A. Reed (OSU), served as a finance analyst at NASDAQ-listed CAAS Inc., and taught Statistics at Ohio State.
