My work spans Neuromorphic Computing (improving language models by drawing inspiration from human cognition), AI Consciousness, and AI Interpretability. Most recently, I designed AMOR, a hybrid architecture inspired by Daniel Kahneman's Thinking, Fast and Slow — pairing a fast, intuitive recurrent model with a slow, deliberate attention mechanism, gated by the model entropy (advised by Dr. Chen Shani – Stanford). More on my Google Scholar.

In past lives, I did my Master's in Data Science at The University of Chicago (GPA 4.0), triple-majored in Economics, Finance, and History at The Ohio State University (top 1% Honors), worked as a research assistant in Behavioral Science (Dr. Nick Epley – UChicago) and in History (Dr. Christopher A. Reed – OSU), served as a finance analyst at NASDAQ-listed CAAS Inc., and taught Statistics at Ohio State.
